Our class was recently introduced to the innovative Fietskar project by De Reuringdienst, a modular and mobile frame designed to bring social issues directly to the public. This exciting initiative challenges us to think beyond traditional approaches and engage audiences in a fun and interactive way.
The Fietskar's versatility lies in its ability to be customized with various modules. This allows us to tailor the experience to different social goals, providing informative and engaging experiences that spark conversation and raise awareness.
As part of this project, we'll be tasked with designing, building, and testing interactive modules that address a chosen social issue. This hands-on experience will not only allow us to hone our creative and technical skills but also contribute to a meaningful project that has the potential to make a real difference in our communities.

My vision is for technology that can connects people across language barriers, fostering social interaction and promoting physical activity.

Value Proposition üîÉ


The focus now shifts to crafting a compelling value proposition for our laser maze project. This involves clearly articulating the benefits it offers to the target audience. C4 Model üìä


Building on the 3D model and the solidified concept, we'll take the next step by delving into the C4 model. This well-established framework will help us visualize the software architecture of the laser maze project. We'll begin by creating level 1 and level 2 of the C4 model. Level 1, the System Context diagram, will provide a high-level overview of the project, showcasing how it interacts with external systems and users. Level 2, the Container diagram, will decompose the laser maze system into its core components, such as the laser control unit, sensor system, and user interface. By establishing a clear software architecture through the C4 model, we can ensure a well-organized and maintainable codebase as we move forward with development.

Multiple Rapid Prototypes ‚úÇÔ∏è


Fueled by the insightful feedback from our Oud-woensel interviews and the comprehensive research conducted, we're now ready to embark on the exciting phase of crafting the prototype.



In our initial prototype, we utilized grass cord to represent the laser beams within the maze. However, after seeking feedback from Mijke, we pivoted to a more aesthetically pleasing solution. Red string replaced the grass cord, instantly enhancing the visual appeal of the laser beams. To further improve the user experience, we incorporated a paper roof for the maze. This addressed the functionality of the tent enclosure from the 3D model, allowing the red string "laser beams" to be clearly visible even in outdoor settings.

Capping off our second sprint, we presented our laser maze demo at De Reuringdienst. The feedback session proved invaluable, yielding a wealth of suggestions and questions that will guide our future development.

Several comments highlighted the importance of fostering a sense of playfulness and rekindling childhood wonder. Feedback also touched on the possibility of incorporating a communication disruption element, perhaps linked to social media, to further engage the target audience. The importance of narrative and connecting with the target group's interests was emphasized.
