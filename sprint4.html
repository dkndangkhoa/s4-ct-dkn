Poster explaining project üéà


After wrapping up user testing in Breda, it's clear we need some solid branding materials to properly introduce our project. Here's why:
Information Gap: We saw some confusion during testing. A booklet and poster could explain the purpose of our research (our study) and the motivation behind the laser maze itself.
Branding Boost: While the participants dug the maze and our vision for the final product, a poster could really grab attention and build hype. We could showcase the laser maze concept and get people excited about potentially seeing it on the streets.





Srpint 4Ô∏è‚É£ Demo üßëüèª‚Äçüíª


Capping off our second sprint, we presented our laser maze demo at De Reuringdienst. The feedback session proved invaluable, yielding a wealth of suggestions and questions that will guide our future development.
Questions centered around enhancing user engagement. Could a treasure be hidden within the maze? Perhaps a tangible reward for completion? Identifying the ideal target group and strategies to draw people outdoors were also key points of discussion. The suggestion to leverage social media to generate excitement resonated strongly, prompting us to consider how to best utilize these platforms to motivate participation. Integrating a game element was another intriguing suggestion, potentially connecting the maze to a larger experience.
The feedback itself provided a treasure trove of ideas. The ice cream sound effect, for instance, offered a creative way to entice teenagers outdoors. The concept of a city-wide trigger that activates the maze introduced an element of surprise and intrigue. Partnering with a school or sports teacher was another valuable suggestion, allowing us to tailor the maze's difficulty to specific age groups and physical capabilities. Several comments highlighted the importance of fostering a sense of playfulness and rekindling childhood wonder. Feedback also touched on the possibility of incorporating a communication disruption element, perhaps linked to social media, to further engage the target audience. The importance of narrative and connecting with the target group's interests was emphasized.
