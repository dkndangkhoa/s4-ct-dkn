Connected proofs of concept with either code or Wizard of Oz üîÆ


When receiving enough technical components (Lasers, sensors), we imidiately start to connect and set up based on the tech research and low-fi prototype we made before.
At the beginning, we decided to connect every lasers with wires but after set it up together it is impossible to build it within the tent as the length of these wires is not long enough, so we decided to build a small base for each laser. We started with a 3D design that play a role as a frame for laser ans sensor. A small power bank will serve as a electrical source.
Technologies use:
Laser diode 5v Module rode laser 650nm 5mW (20) - We choose this instead of higher power laser with two main reasons: affordable price and safety use (as our target audiences is from age 12 to 18).
Smoke machine.
5v powerbank.
Raspberry Pi 4 - To connect and controls lasers and sensors via wifi.

Tests for prototype (both technical and with target group) üö©


After duscussing, we decided to test our first prototype in a school in Breda (Christoffel Breda).
Unfortunately, after setting up the tent, because brightness, the lasers was not visible even with smoke machine. Therefore, we decided to use red strings as a temporary solution. Luckily, user testing went well, children was really excited to participate and playing around with it.








Poster explaining project üéà


After wrapping up user testing in Breda, it's clear we need some solid branding materials to properly introduce our project. Here's why:
Information Gap: We saw some confusion during testing. A booklet and poster could explain the purpose of our research (our study) and the motivation behind the laser maze itself.
Branding Boost: While the participants dug the maze and our vision for the final product, a poster could really grab attention and build hype. We could showcase the laser maze concept and get people excited about potentially seeing it on the streets.
