Smart Parking 🚘



Project type:

Individual

Started :

February 2024

Completed :

March 2024

Have you ever spent precious time circling for a parking spot, contributing to traffic congestion and burning fuel in the process? This project proposes a revolutionary solution: a smart parking system with a user-friendly mobile app that will transform your parking experience.
This introduction sets the stage for your innovative project by highlighting the common pain point of finding parking and introduces the concept of a user-centric solution. It piques the reader's interest by posing a relatable question and then immediately offering a solution.






Research 📖



To tackle this challenge head-on, I'm starting this project with a deep dive into research. Understanding user needs and the existing parking landscape is crucial. Through this research, I aim to develop a system that is not only technologically sound, but also addresses the real-world frustrations faced by drivers.


Idea 💡


This project outlines the development of a smart parking system with a user-friendly mobile app. The system aims to improve the parking experience for drivers by providing real-time information on available spaces, enabling booking and cashless payments, and reducing traffic congestion.


Project vision 🔭


This project envisions a future where drivers can navigate traffic-free streets, eliminate the frustration of circling for parking, and contribute to a greener city by reducing emissions from idling vehicles. This smart parking system will achieve this by:
• Optimizing parking utilization: Real-time data will guide drivers to available spaces, reducing wasted time searching.
• Streamlining payments: Mobile app integration will allow for cashless and contactless payment for parking fees.
• Enhancing traffic flow: By reducing time spent searching for parking, overall traffic congestion will be minimized.

Brainstorm results 🧠


"What if we could use FietsKar to motivate people to exercise more?"

As we gathered for the project kickoff, the air crackled with excitement and a flurry of ideas. Each member began brainstorming potential directions, eager to contribute their unique perspective. I, drawn to the intersection of technology and well-being, decided to delve deeper into four intriguing areas: the immersive potential of Virtual Reality Exercise, the personalized guidance of AI-Powered Fitness Assistants, the stress-busting benefits of Exercise and stress relief, and the injury prevention capabilities of Wearable Technology.

Virtual Reality Exercise: This burgeoning field offers a captivating and immersive way to engage in physical activity. Wearable Technology in Injury Prevention: Wearable devices can play a crucial role in preventing injuries. By monitoring vital signs, analyzing movement patterns, and providing feedback, these technologies can help users stay safe and optimize their training.

By researching these four areas, I aimed to gain a comprehensive understanding of the latest advancements and identify potential applications within the Fietskar project.



Project Plan with CTPD 📃


With the initial brainstorming complete, we delved deeper into the creative technology design process. Our focus shifted to identifying the "trigger" – the abstract or concrete element that would spark the laser maze experience and engage users. This involved delving into the research on factors that contribute to low physical activity levels. We explored various avenues, including the potential of using elements of gamification, social interaction, and personalized feedback to address common barriers like lack of motivation, time constraints, and perceived lack of enjoyment. Through this process, we aimed to pinpoint the most effective trigger, one that would not only initiate the laser maze experience but also resonate with users and encourage them to actively participate.




Vision statement 🔭


Our research indicates a critical need for technology that tackles the growing issues of physical inactivity and social isolation among adolescents (12-18 years old). Studies by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) [1] reveal a concerning decline in physical activity among this age group, leading to increased health risks. Furthermore, a recent Pew Research Center report [2] highlights the rise of social media use and its potential to contribute to feelings of loneliness and disconnection.

By leveraging these insights, we envision technology that bridges the gap between physical exercise and social interaction. This technology will create a fun and engaging environment where adolescents can be physically active while simultaneously fostering social connections with their peers. Through innovative features and gamification elements, we aim to motivate teenagers to embrace a healthy lifestyle and build meaningful social bonds.

Sources:

[1] Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).


[2] Pew Research Center.




Problem statement 📝


The current landscape for teenagers presents a concerning trend: a decline in physical activity coupled with a rise in social media use that can contribute to feelings of isolation. This lack of physical engagement and meaningful social interaction can negatively impact their overall well-being.

Existing solutions often fall short. Traditional fitness routines can be monotonous and lack social engagement, while social media platforms, while fostering connection, often do little to encourage physical activity. This dichotomy necessitates a novel approach.

There is a clear need for technology that effectively addresses this dual challenge. We propose developing a solution that motivates teenagers to be physically active, while simultaneously fostering meaningful social connections with their peers. This technology will bridge the gap between physical exercise and social interaction, promoting a healthier and more fulfilling lifestyle for adolescents.

My initial idea is creating a technology that attaches to a Fietskar, transforming any bike into an exercise bike. This would enable teenagers to compete together and exercise more, even if they're physically distant. Imagine a mobile app that connects riders and allows them to see each other's progress on a virtual track. We could even incorporate leaderboards and challenges to add a layer of friendly competition and keep teenagers motivated.

I have received a lot of feedback and suggestions from my teammates that have really enriched this initial concept. For example, Tijmen suggested adding augmented reality elements to the virtual track, creating a more immersive experience for riders. Rik proposed incorporating social features like voice chat, allowing teenagers to connect and motivate each other during their workouts. These insights have helped us envision a more comprehensive technology that not only encourages exercise but also fosters a strong sense of community among teenagers.



All members ideas


After our discussion, we ultimately gravitated towards Pjotr's idea of utilizing a laser beam to create a collaborative maze game. This concept sparked excitement as it offered a unique way for teenagers to exercise and socialize simultaneously. We envisioned a laser maze course that could be set up in various locations, encouraging groups to work together to navigate the beams while strategizing and communicating effectively. This not only promotes physical activity but also hones teamwork and problem-solving skills in a fun and interactive environment.



Concept board 🎨


After choosing Pjotr's idea of a Fietskar Laser Maze, we turned our attention to creating a compelling concept board. Leveraging my background in media, I designed a board centered around the tagline "It's that easy to..." The image would be eye-catching, perhaps showcasing a group of teenagers laughing and strategizing as they navigate a brightly lit laser maze course with their Fietskar bikes. This tagline and image combination would instantly capture the project's essence – a seemingly complex activity made simple and enjoyable through innovative technology and teamwork.




Target group research ⭕


We continued with target group research to refine our concept. Leveraging statistics from the Eindhoven government, we identified a specific demographic to focus on: 12-18 year olds residing in the Oud-Woensel neighborhood. This data allowed us to tailor our laser maze game concept to better address the needs and interests of this particular group. By understanding their preferred activities and social environments, we can create a more engaging and impactful experience that effectively promotes exercise and social interaction within the Oud-Woensel community.

- Storyboards

In This sprint, I am mainly responsible for doing Sketches, Storyboards, Visual Prototyping and Multiple Rapid Prototyping.


Project Plan with CTPD 📃


Adhering to the Creative Technology Process Design method, we embarked on the Ideation phase for the fietcar project. Here, we unleashed our creativity, each team member striving to generate at least three ideas – both abstract and concrete – that utilized laser beams. Following a collaborative discussion, we arrived at a unanimous decision: a laser maze! This concept resonated with the group for its potential to create an engaging and interactive experience.




Sketches ✏️


Taking the concept we brainstormed as a foundation, I translate it into a visual starting point with an initial sketch. This rough draft captures the essence of the idea, allowing us to explore its potential and identify any initial challenges. It's a springboard for further discussion and refinement before diving into more detailed visuals.



To further refine our approach, we employed a mindmap process. This brainstorming technique allowed us to visually explore key aspects of the project. The mindmap focused on three critical areas: target group, reasons for testing, and the ideal location for our prototype.




Storyboards 🖼️


Following the collaborative brainstorming sessions, I took the lead on translating our vision for the laser maze project into a visual narrative. Through the creation of a storyboard, I aimed to capture the user journey – from entering the maze to successfully navigating its challenges. This storyboard will serve as a roadmap, guiding the development process and ensuring all team members share a clear understanding of the desired user experience.




Visual Prototyping ✨


Integrating the valuable insights gleaned from the sketch and storyboard feedback, I'm now crafting a 3D model for the laser maze. This digital representation will depict the entire experience, including a tent enclosure. The tent serves a crucial purpose: by creating a controlled environment, it allows the laser beams to be visible outdoors during daylight hours. This attention to detail ensures the laser maze can be enjoyed in a wider range of settings, enhancing its overall usability.




Tech Research ⚙️


Armed with a solidified concept and a user-centric storyboard, we transitioned seamlessly into the technical research phase. Here, we delved deeper into the technological aspects of the laser maze project. Our focus shifted towards researching the most suitable components: exploring different types of lasers, sensors for detecting breaks in the beam, and potential microcontrollers to manage the maze's logic.

Tech Research Document.



Value Proposition 🔃


The focus now shifts to crafting a compelling value proposition for our laser maze project. This involves clearly articulating the benefits it offers to the target audience. We'll explore questions like: How will the laser maze be fun and engaging? What educational value can it provide? Does it address a specific need or desire within the target group? By defining a strong value proposition, we can ensure the project resonates with users and sets it apart from similar experiences.




C4 Model 📊


Building on the 3D model and the solidified concept, we'll take the next step by delving into the C4 model. This well-established framework will help us visualize the software architecture of the laser maze project. We'll begin by creating level 1 and level 2 of the C4 model. Level 1, the System Context diagram, will provide a high-level overview of the project, showcasing how it interacts with external systems and users. Level 2, the Container diagram, will decompose the laser maze system into its core components, such as the laser control unit, sensor system, and user interface. By establishing a clear software architecture through the C4 model, we can ensure a well-organized and maintainable codebase as we move forward with development.

Interview 🎥


Recognizing the value of further feedback, we decided to take a proactive approach and gather additional insights from potential users in the Oud-woensel area. To maximize efficiency, we split into two groups: myself, Tijmen, and Pjotr in one, and Elene and Rik in the other. Full Interview Document.


Multiple Rapid Prototypes ✂️


Fueled by the insightful feedback from our Oud-woensel interviews and the comprehensive research conducted, we're now ready to embark on the exciting phase of crafting the prototype.



In our initial prototype, we utilized grass cord to represent the laser beams within the maze. However, after seeking feedback from Mijke, we pivoted to a more aesthetically pleasing solution. Red string replaced the grass cord, instantly enhancing the visual appeal of the laser beams. To further improve the user experience, we incorporated a paper roof for the maze. This addressed the functionality of the tent enclosure from the 3D model, allowing the red string "laser beams" to be clearly visible even in outdoor settings.
