Project Plan with CTPD üìÉ


Adhering to the Creative Technology Process Design method, we embarked on the Ideation phase for the fietcar project. Here, we unleashed our creativity, each team member striving to generate at least three ideas ‚Äì both abstract and concrete ‚Äì that utilized laser beams. Following a collaborative discussion, we arrived at a unanimous decision: a laser maze! This concept resonated with the group for its potential to create an engaging and interactive experience.




Sketches ‚úèÔ∏è


Taking the concept we brainstormed as a foundation, I translate it into a visual starting point with an initial sketch. This rough draft captures the essence of the idea, allowing us to explore its potential and identify any initial challenges. It's a springboard for further discussion and refinement before diving into more detailed visuals.



To further refine our approach, we employed a mindmap process. This brainstorming technique allowed us to visually explore key aspects of the project. The mindmap focused on three critical areas: target group, reasons for testing, and the ideal location for our prototype.




Storyboards üñºÔ∏è


Following the collaborative brainstorming sessions, I took the lead on translating our vision for the laser maze project into a visual narrative. Through the creation of a storyboard, I aimed to capture the user journey ‚Äì from entering the maze to successfully navigating its challenges. This storyboard will serve as a roadmap, guiding the development process and ensuring all team members share a clear understanding of the desired user experience.




Visual Prototyping ‚ú®


Integrating the valuable insights gleaned from the sketch and storyboard feedback, I'm now crafting a 3D model for the laser maze. This digital representation will depict the entire experience, including a tent enclosure. The tent serves a crucial purpose: by creating a controlled environment, it allows the laser beams to be visible outdoors during daylight hours. This attention to detail ensures the laser maze can be enjoyed in a wider range of settings, enhancing its overall usability.




Tech Research ‚öôÔ∏è


Armed with a solidified concept and a user-centric storyboard, we transitioned seamlessly into the technical research phase. Here, we delved deeper into the technological aspects of the laser maze project. Our focus shifted towards researching the most suitable components: exploring different types of lasers, sensors for detecting breaks in the beam, and potential microcontrollers to manage the maze's logic.

Tech Research Document.



Value Proposition üîÉ


The focus now shifts to crafting a compelling value proposition for our laser maze project. This involves clearly articulating the benefits it offers to the target audience. We'll explore questions like: How will the laser maze be fun and engaging? What educational value can it provide? Does it address a specific need or desire within the target group? By defining a strong value proposition, we can ensure the project resonates with users and sets it apart from similar experiences.




C4 Model üìä


Building on the 3D model and the solidified concept, we'll take the next step by delving into the C4 model. This well-established framework will help us visualize the software architecture of the laser maze project. We'll begin by creating level 1 and level 2 of the C4 model. Level 1, the System Context diagram, will provide a high-level overview of the project, showcasing how it interacts with external systems and users. Level 2, the Container diagram, will decompose the laser maze system into its core components, such as the laser control unit, sensor system, and user interface. By establishing a clear software architecture through the C4 model, we can ensure a well-organized and maintainable codebase as we move forward with development.

Multiple Rapid Prototypes ‚úÇÔ∏è


Fueled by the insightful feedback from our Oud-woensel interviews and the comprehensive research conducted, we're now ready to embark on the exciting phase of crafting the prototype.



In our initial prototype, we utilized grass cord to represent the laser beams within the maze. However, after seeking feedback from Mijke, we pivoted to a more aesthetically pleasing solution. Red string replaced the grass cord, instantly enhancing the visual appeal of the laser beams. To further improve the user experience, we incorporated a paper roof for the maze. This addressed the functionality of the tent enclosure from the 3D model, allowing the red string "laser beams" to be clearly visible even in outdoor settings.
